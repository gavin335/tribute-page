Dwight D. Eisenhower

Dwight D. Eisenhower

  • 1890 - Born on October 14th in Denison, Texas
  • 1892 - Family moved to Abilene, Kansas, which he considered his hometown
  • 1911 - Got accepted to West Point, where he starred on the football team as running back and linebacker
  • 1916 - While stationed in Texas, he married Mamie Doud. They eventually had two sons
  • 1918 - Commanded a unit that trained tank crews in Pennsylvania. They were ordered to go to France to fight in World War I, but the war ended a week before their departure
  • 1926 - Graduated first in his class at Command and General Staff College in Kansas
  • 1935 - As General Douglas MacArthur's chief military aide, traveled to the Philippines to help develop their army
  • 1942 - Was named Supreme Allied Commander for the North African theater of World War II
  • 1943 - Was promoted to Supreme Allied Commander of Europe
  • 1944 - Commanded the Allies as they stormed the beaches of Normandy, France on D-Day
  • 1945 - Following World War II, was named Chief of Staff of the Army
  • 1948 - Became president of Columbia University in New York City
  • 1952 - Running as a Republican, he was elected President of the United States in a landslide victory over Adlai Stevenson II
  • 1953 - Helped end the Korean War
  • 1956 - Was re-elected as President in another landslide against the same opponent
  • 1957 - Signed the Civil Rights Act of 1957 into law. Deployed troops to Arkansas when a school refused to de-segregate their classes
  • 1957 - Established NASA
  • 1961 - In his final speech to the public as President, he warned against the military-industrial complex
  • 1969 - Died in Washington, DC of heart failure
